# Comparisons

## ETG vs IAM
IAM controls who can call systems.
ETG controls whether a specific irreversible action is allowed to commit under explicit, verifiable constraints.

## ETG vs Observability
Observability explains what happened.
ETG prevents unauthorized execution and produces portable evidence of what constraints were active.

## ETG vs Policy-as-code
Policy-as-code defines rules.
ETG defines how a decision is represented, bound, verified, and audited at execution time.

## ETG vs Prompt safety
Prompt safety influences model behavior.
ETG constrains execution regardless of model output.
